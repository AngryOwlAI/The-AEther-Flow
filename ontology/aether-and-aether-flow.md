## Æther and Æther-Flow

This note states the front-facing ontology that underlies `The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity`. It is an ontology statement, not a claim that the project has already completed a first-principles substrate derivation of gravity. Its role is to say clearly what the theory is about before the exact relativistic package states what is adopted mathematically.

### 1. Ontological Core

The project begins from the claim that reality is grounded in a deeper four-dimensional substrate called the `Æther`. The `Æther-flow` is the intrinsic ordered motion of that substrate. On this view, the familiar world of observation is not the deepest available level of description, but the observer-accessible appearance of a deeper order.

The core vocabulary is:

- `Æther`: the underlying four-dimensional substrate of reality
- `Æther-flow`: the intrinsic ordered motion of that substrate
- observed three-dimensional space: the local experiential slice of the deeper substrate
- `S-time`: the experienced order of change arising from matter, light, and the `Æther-flow`
- observed expansion: the three-dimensional appearance of deeper four-dimensional ordered motion

This vocabulary is meant to discipline the ontology. The `Æther` should not be pictured as an ordinary three-dimensional medium expanding into an external void, and the `Æther-flow` should not be reduced casually to a simple wind, river, or current in observed space.

### 2. Observed Space and `S-time`

Observed three-dimensional space is the local experiential slice through which embedded observers encounter the deeper substrate. What is measured as distance, extension, and spatial separation belongs to that observer-level slice rather than exhausting the ontology itself.

`S-time` names the experienced order of change. It is not a second spatial corridor or a container through which the world travels. It is the ordered relation among matter, light, and the `Æther-flow` as registered by physical systems. The point of this language is to preserve the project's original intuition that time is bound up with change while avoiding the unstable image of time as a hidden place-like direction inside the substrate.

### 3. Observed Expansion and Relativistic Phenomena

Within this ontology, observed expansion is treated as phenomenological appearance rather than as primary ontology. The claim is not that expansion is unreal. The claim is that what observers describe as expansion is the three-dimensional appearance of a deeper four-dimensional ordered motion.

The same caution applies to relativistic phenomena. Time dilation, length contraction, redshift, and light propagation are not denied. They are part of the measurable observer-level structure that the project must account for. Ontology concerns what that structure is about; it does not by itself replace the exact mathematical law governing those phenomena.

### 4. Gravity as Mass-Shaped Reorganization

At the ontology level, gravity may be interpreted as the observer-level effect of matter locally reorganizing the surrounding `Æther-flow`. The most useful intuitive picture is not a uniform current but a convergent or vortical reshaping of the deeper ordered motion around mass.

This should be read heuristically and carefully. The claim is not that observed three-dimensional space is literally an ordinary fluid. The claim is that gravitational attraction may be interpreted as a structured response of the deeper substrate to matter, with familiar relativistic gravitational phenomena appearing at the observer level through that reorganization.

The ontology remains underselective at this level. To become a derivation rather than an interpretation compatible with exact GR, it would need a substrate-level observer-response or equivalence-locking law that forces observer soldering, full same-metric matter with mode control, and S-time / Einsteinian closure together.

The front-facing ontological claim of the project is straightforward. The `Æther` is the deeper four-dimensional substrate of reality. The `Æther-flow` is its intrinsic ordered motion. Observed three-dimensional space is the local experiential slice of that deeper substrate. `S-time` is the experienced order of change arising from matter, light, and the `Æther-flow`.
