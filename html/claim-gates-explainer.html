Three-layer model

High Level, Operational, Evidence

High-Level Model

Purpose

Claim gates prevent workflow progress from being mistaken for accepted physics.

Candidate status

A candidate may be useful, blocked, repaired, or refuted without being promoted.

Negative results

Obstructions are preserved because they constrain future work.

Human gates

Certain promotions require Gate Chair or human-gated review.

Operational Model

Allowed claims

Each task can say only what its boundary allows.

Forbidden claims

Derivation, benchmark promotion, canonical adoption, and target import claims are blocked unless gated.

Artifacts

Task outputs and TeX registry rows preserve scoped results.

Handoffs

Next work inherits explicit exclusions and obstructions.

Claim Boundary

Premise
Claim boundaries prevent candidate or workflow status from becoming accepted physics by implication.
Mechanism
Rows record allowed claims, forbidden claims, required gates, and authority source paths.
Source Basis
CLAIM_BOUNDARY_REGISTRY.csv, AGENTS.md, and research_control/README.md.
Authority Status
Human-only explanation of existing behavior.
Uncertainty
A candidate may be locally useful even when promotion is blocked.
Validation Or Test
Inspect the claim-boundary row before accepting any derivation or benchmark statement.
Next Step
Check whether the claim requires Gate Chair or human-gated review.

Negative Result

Premise
Negative results are preserved because they prevent repeated failed routes.
Mechanism
A failed route can become a registered artifact, boundary row, completion receipt, and handoff constraint.
Source Basis
TEX_SOURCE_REGISTRY.csv, RESEARCH_TASK_REGISTRY.csv, and CLAIM_BOUNDARY_REGISTRY.csv.
Authority Status
Scoped obstruction explanation, not a global theory verdict.
Uncertainty
A local refutation can block one route without rejecting the entire program.
Validation Or Test
Verify scope, task path, authority source, forbidden promotions, and handoff constraints.
Next Step
Use source authority to inspect registration and derivative boundaries.
