Three-layer model

High Level, Operational, Evidence

High-Level Model

Role as boundary

A role is not a personality label; it is an authority and output boundary.

Director selection

The Director maps task type and source authority to a role candidate.

Execution-role record

The job-local role record is the actual contract for that task.

Expiry

Task overlays and provisional roles expire with the AgentJob unless human-authorized registration occurs.

Operational Model

Identify source class

Science, project control, validation, memory, or explanatory documentation.

Compare role fit

Accept or reject candidate roles with reasons.

Bind constraints

Allowed writes, removed permissions, expanded permissions, and validators are recorded.

Execute one job

The role acts only inside the AgentJob boundary.

Role Selection

Premise
Choosing a role is choosing an authority boundary for one task.
Mechanism
The Director compares source class, objective, role contracts, allowed writes, validators, and claim boundaries.
Source Basis
research_control/README.md, AGENT_ROLE_REGISTRY.csv, and DIRECTOR_DECISION_REGISTRY.csv.
Authority Status
Workflow explanation only.
Uncertainty
A template may need a bounded task overlay or provisional one-job identity.
Validation Or Test
Check the Director decision, execution-role record, and AgentJob allowlist.
Next Step
Inspect the task-local execution-role file before executing or reviewing work.

Execution Role

Premise
The execution-role record prevents silent authority drift.
Mechanism
It binds role kind, added constraints, removed permissions, expanded permissions, allowed writes, gates, and expiry.
Source Basis
ROLE_EXECUTION_REGISTRY.csv and EXECUTION_ROLE_SCHEMA.md.
Authority Status
Project-control explanation.
Uncertainty
Repeated provisional patterns may indicate a future role-design task.
Validation Or Test
Validate registry rows against task-local role records and AgentJob fields.
Next Step
Use the research-system explainer to trace the role through completion and handoff.
