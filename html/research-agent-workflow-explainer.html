Workflow process

Research-Agent Workflow

The project develops a staged-autonomy physics/math research-agent harness by running bounded research tasks against the hard GR-derivation problem. Current operation remains human-accountable, role-bounded, and validator-gated.

Workflow status is operational evidence about the research system, not a proof of the physics theory.

Loop Lanes Analysis Sources Claim boundary

One Bounded Execution Loop

1. Director decision

Selects one role and one task boundary.

Inspect step

Inputs: tracked state and registries. Outputs: DDR and selected AgentJob boundary. Authority: project or research director.

2. Role binding

Binds work to a registered role or explicit task overlay.

Inspect step

Inputs: role contracts. Outputs: role execution record. Authority: role registry and task allowlist.

3. AgentJob

Defines allowed reads, writes, outputs, and validators.

Inspect step

Inputs: DDR and role boundary. Outputs: strict YAML job contract. Validator concern: allowed paths and required checks.

4. Bounded output

Produces artifacts without widening authority.

Inspect step

Inputs: job scope. Outputs: source edits, generated derivatives, or task artifacts. Boundary: no physics promotion by workflow status.

5. Validation

Runs deterministic checks before checkpointing.

Inspect step

Inputs: live diff and registries. Outputs: pass/fail receipts. Authority: validator scripts, not model judgment.

6. Completion or handoff

Records result and next boundary.

Inspect step

Inputs: validation results. Outputs: completion YAML or handoff. Boundary: next work remains bounded.

Two Operating Lanes

continue-research

Continues physics research from tracked handoffs and task state. It may route science roles, but does not repair project-system machinery unless explicitly authorized.

improve-project-system

Maintains roles, validators, memory tooling, source-backed explainers, and documentation-impact receipts. It must not promote physics claims.
