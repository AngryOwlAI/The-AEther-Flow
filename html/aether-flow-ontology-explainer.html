Summary of Æther-flow Ontology

The AEther-flow ontology is the project's conceptual vocabulary for a proposed deeper four-dimensional substrate, its intrinsic ordered motion, and the observer-accessible appearance of space, time-order, expansion, and gravity. Its role is not to replace general relativity or claim a completed derivation; it frames exact GR as the observable benchmark that any future substrate law must recover. The ontology matters because it keeps the research program's intuitive picture disciplined: observed three-dimensional space is treated as a local experiential slice, S-time as the experienced order of change, and gravity as a heuristic matter-shaped reorganization of the deeper flow. At the project level, the explainer helps readers separate ontology, mathematical model, benchmark adoption, and open derivation burden before reading diagrams or candidate arguments.

Ontology means the answer to a basic question: what does this project say exists beneath the appearances we measure? In this repository, the answer is not an old static ether or a wind blowing through ordinary space. It is a proposed deeper four-dimensional substrate, named Æther, whose intrinsic ordered motion, named Æther-flow, is used as a conceptual picture for observed space, experienced time-order, expansion, and gravity. The safe current claim is narrower than the picture: ordinary exact GR remains the benchmark at the observable level, while a first-principles derivation from substrate structure remains open. This orientation helps lay readers use the page as a map without mistaking the map for a proof.

Ontology Terms

The core terms are project-local. Æther names the proposed four-dimensional substrate; Æther-flow names its intrinsic ordered motion or relational organization. Observed three-dimensional space is the local experiential slice in which observers measure distance, while S-time is the order of change registered by matter, light, and the flow. Observed expansion is treated as the appearance of deeper ordered motion, and gravity is a heuristic description of matter-shaped reorganization of the surrounding flow. Exact GR Benchmark

README.mdAGENTS.mdontology/aether-and-aether-flow.mdontology/aether_flow_interpretation-lemen.md

Adoption and derivation do different jobs. Adoption means the current operational mathematics remains ordinary exact general relativity: field equations, predictive phenomena, clocks, and observed gravitational behavior are preserved as the benchmark. Derivation would be stronger. It would require explicit substrate laws that recover Lorentzian geometry, causal structure, clocks, universal same-metric matter coupling, invariance, and Einsteinian closure without inserting those target structures by hand. The ontology can be compatible with the benchmark as an interpretation, but compatibility is not uniqueness and not proof. This distinction is the page's central scientific safety rail: the generated explainer may clarify the research target, but only a valid derivation plus claim-gate review could change scientific status.
