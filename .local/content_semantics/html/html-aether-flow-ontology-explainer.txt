Æther Flow Ontology Explainer
Conceptual model
Æther / Æther-Flow Ontology
This page explains what the project means by ontology: a proposed substrate-and-flow framing that may guide a derivation, but is not itself a completed derivation of general relativity.
The accepted benchmark remains ordinary GR at observable scale. The open problem is to recover that benchmark from source-defined substrate structure without importing the target metric by hand.

Ontology-to-Benchmark Stack
1. Substrate idea
Æther
names the proposed deeper substrate.
What this can do
It can organize hypotheses and constraints. It cannot by itself prove GR or replace registered scientific sources.
2. Flow organization
Æther-flow
names structured relational organization in that substrate.
What must be specified
Admissible variables, evolution rules, invariants, dimensions, boundary conditions, and matter coupling.
3. Effective behavior
The target is emergent relativistic behavior with clocks, causal structure, and universal coupling.
What must not happen
The derivation must not smuggle in the Lorentzian metric, target proper time, or observer protocol as hidden inputs.
4. Exact-GR benchmark
The conservative public benchmark remains ordinary GR at observable scale.
What remains open
Recovering the benchmark from first principles remains unresolved until authorized gates establish it.
