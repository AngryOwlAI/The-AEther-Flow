# Roles and Skills

The roles-and-skills system explains who may do what kind of work inside a bounded transaction.

Authority boundary: this page is a readable map. It does not register roles, change permissions, alter skill contracts, or authorize scientific claim promotion.

## Roles

Registered roles are stable templates for Director reasoning. Execution happens through one of three forms:

- `registered_role`: the role contract fits the task without change.
- `task_overlay`: the registered role remains the correct identity but receives a bounded task-specific permission delta.
- `one_job_provisional_role`: a temporary one-job role or role variant is needed.

Recurring provisional-role patterns are not silently normalized. They route through project-system improvement for possible human-authorized registration.

## Current Role Lanes

| Lane | Examples | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research operations | Director of Research, Project-System Director, Project-Control Maintainer, Documentation Curator, Validator Engineer, Memory-System Maintainer. | Workflow, validation, documentation, memory, and control machinery. |
| Physics roles | Ontology Formalizer, Candidate Constructor, Refuter, Smuggling Auditor, Gate Chair. | Scientific artifacts and review under claim boundaries. |
| Task overlays | One-job expansions or restrictions. | Expire after the AgentJob. |

## Repo-Local Skills

Repo-local skills under [../../.codex/skills/](../../.codex/skills/) are project-governed procedures. They include continuation, project-system improvement, memory/wiki operations, HTML explainers, Mermaid documentation, TeX/PDF derivative handling, ontology promotion, and design interrogation.

Global Codex tools, browser tools, editors, shells, and local plugins can help an operator, but they do not become project authority unless mirrored into tracked project sources.
