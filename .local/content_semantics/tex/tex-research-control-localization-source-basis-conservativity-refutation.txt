\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Conservativity Refutation}\\[0.35em]
\large Source-Language Test for the Source-Basis Packet}
\author{Refuter AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-009-001}
\date{June 8, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft tests whether the localization source-basis packet is conservative
over registered source language. The result is local and negative. The packet
does not directly import a target metric, target null cone, radar protocol, or
target proper-time normalization. However, the added basis structures
\(\Step\), \(\Adj\), \(\mathcal{P}\), \(\mathcal{D}\), \(\RespAlg\), and
\(\mathcal{K}\) are not yet forced by the registered source reservoir. Distinct
admissible extensions can agree on the registered source content while
disagreeing on localization-relevant fronts, ranks, and clock transports.
Candidate reconstruction and Gate Chair review therefore remain blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Refutation Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260608-009}. It
does not edit canonical ontology TeX, issue a Gate Chair verdict, promote a
candidate, or reject the broader \TheoryProgram{}. It attacks the source-basis
repair path after the smuggling audit found that structural target import
remains possible.

Let \(\Lang\) denote the currently registered source language available to this
task: the Gate 0 source primitives, the localization repair/refutation chain,
and the draft source-basis formalization as a proposed extension. Let
\[
\Ext =
(\Step,\Adj,\mathcal{P},\mathcal{D},\{\RespAlg\}_{\Gamma},\mathcal{K})
\]
denote the additional basis data introduced to make localization testable.

\begin{test}[Local source conservativity]
The source-basis packet is locally conservative over \(\Lang\) only if every
localization-relevant conclusion used by a later candidate is invariant under
all admissible \(\Ext\)-expansions that agree on the registered \(\Lang\)
content. If two admissible expansions agree on \(\Lang\) but disagree on
front incidence, adjacency neighborhoods, response rank, or clock transport,
then the packet has added nonconservative structure.
\end{test}

This standard is intentionally weaker than a full model-theoretic theorem. It
is sufficient for a control refutation because the next candidate would rely
on exactly these structures.

\section{Transition and Adjacency Nonconservativity}

\begin{refutationfinding}[Immediate transition is independent data]
The source-basis packet defines \(\Step\) as an immediate transition relation
compatible with source order. Compatibility with order does not determine
immediacy. The same ordered substrate can support many nonisomorphic
immediate-transition graphs with different path lengths, neighborhoods, and
boundaries.
\end{refutationfinding}

\begin{countermodel}[Two compatible transition presentations]
Fix the same ordered source chain and the same relabeling class. One
admissible presentation may connect only consecutive source stages. Another
may connect consecutive stages and selected same-stage response supports.
Both presentations can preserve the same source order, but they generate
different \(\Adj\)-paths, different \(N_n(x)\), and different boundary sets
\(\partial_{\mathcal{S}}R\). A later localization front would therefore
depend on \(\Ext\), not on \(\Lang\) alone.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Topology blocker]
Until \(\Step\) has a source-generation law, or until all admissible
\(\Step\)-presentations are proven localization-equivalent, the adjacency
topology is a nonconservative extension. It may be useful, but it is not yet
derived from registered source language.
\end{blocker}

\section{Perturbation and Discriminator Nonconservativity}

\begin{refutationfinding}[Rank depends on admissibility choices]
The rank object \(r_D(a,b)\) removes the earlier numerical first-response
interval, but it still depends on perturbation classes \(P_a\) and response
discriminators \(D\). The registered source reservoir does not yet determine
which perturbations and discriminators are admissible.
\end{refutationfinding}

\begin{countermodel}[Discriminator split]
For the same \(\Step\)-presentation, one discriminator may count a response
when a recurrence word first changes; another may require a stable repeated
change across two cycles. These discriminators agree that only source records
are used, but they can assign different first-detection ranks. The resulting
front incidence is not invariant across admissible \(\mathcal{D}\) choices.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Response-selection blocker]
The packet requires a source law that fixes \(\mathcal{P}\) and
\(\mathcal{D}\), or a theorem that localization-relevant incidence is
independent of their choice. Without that, the discriminator family is a
discrete tuning channel.
\end{blocker}

\section{Response-Rank Nonconservativity}

\begin{refutationfinding}[Rank four is not forced]
The source-basis packet permits one order mode plus three non-order response
modes when \(\Rank(U_{\Gamma})=4\). The statement is cleaner than an asserted
target \(1+3\) split, but the registered source language does not yet force
rank four. It also does not define a unique response algebra whose dependence
relations fix the rank.
\end{refutationfinding}

\begin{countermodel}[Rank variation]
Two response algebras can be generated from the same observer-chain record:
one quotient may identify two response functionals as source-equivalent,
while another keeps them independent. The first can yield rank three and the
second rank four over the same domain. Both are source-named unless a
stronger source equivalence law selects one. A later four-mode candidate would
therefore depend on a nonconservative choice.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Mode-count blocker]
Candidate reconstruction may not assume a four-dimensional response domain
until the source dynamics determine \(\RespAlg\) and its independence
criterion. Otherwise the packet can reproduce the target count by selection.
\end{blocker}

\section{Clock-Transport Nonconservativity}

\begin{refutationfinding}[Transport cocycle is free structure]
The packet adds clock-cycle equivalence and transport
\(T_{\Gamma\to\Gamma'}\). This addresses the earlier local-scale problem, but
the registered source language does not yet determine a unique transport
cocycle, a holonomy class, or a normalization rule.
\end{refutationfinding}

\begin{countermodel}[Holonomy variation]
Consider the same chain-adjacency graph and the same stable cycle classes.
One admissible transport assignment may preserve cycle ratios around every
closed path. Another may preserve local ratios but produce nontrivial holonomy
around a loop. Both can be phrased as source transport data unless a source
law fixes the cocycle. They yield different cross-chain clock comparisons.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Clock-scale blocker]
A transported source cycle is not yet an effective clock scale. The packet
needs either a source derivation of the transport cocycle or an explicit
admission that \(\mathcal{K}\) is a new ontology-extension hypothesis.
\end{blocker}

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{localverdict}[Source-basis packet is not locally conservative]
The source-basis packet is not locally conservative over the registered source
language as presently stated. Its added structures are scientifically useful
because they expose where localization would need source-side content, but
they are not yet consequences of that content. The nonconservative channels
are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item immediate-transition incidence \(\Step\);
    \item adjacency neighborhoods and source boundaries from \(\Adj\);
    \item perturbation and response-discriminator admissibility;
    \item response algebra and rank-four mode selection; and
    \item clock-cycle transport and holonomy normalization.
\end{enumerate}
\end{localverdict}

This is a local negative result. It blocks candidate reconstruction from the
current source-basis packet, but it does not close the research path.

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Minimal source-basis axiom justification]
The logical next scientific step is not candidate reconstruction. The next
step is either a bounded Ontology Formalizer task that proposes minimal
source-side axioms fixing \(\Step\), \(\mathcal{D}\), \(\RespAlg\), and
\(\mathcal{K}\), or a Director-controlled pause if that would exceed current
authority. The task should distinguish conservative definitional expansion
from an explicit ontology-extension hypothesis.
\end{nextburden}

Gate Chair review remains premature because there is no candidate-ready
source-conservative localization packet.
