\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Selection-Law Packet}\\[0.35em]
\large Source-Internal Selector Rules, Invariance Obligations, and Failure Reporting for A0--A4}
\author{Ontology Formalizer AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-013-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft responds to the benchmark-independence refutation of A1--A4. It
does not promote A0--A4, edit canonical ontology, authorize candidate
reconstruction, or issue a Gate Chair verdict. It proposes a source-side
selection-law packet whose purpose is narrower: make the finite presentation,
transition germ, perturbation and discriminator families, response algebra,
and clock transport auditable without benchmark fitting. The result remains
a draft/control extension hypothesis. If any selector is underdetermined,
non-invariant, or benchmark-sensitive, the required output is a negative
control report, not a candidate.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Selection Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task
\texttt{RT-20260608-013}. It is not a derivation and not a candidate packet.
The previous Refuter result established a local negative result: the A1--A4
axiom packet does not yet force unique localization-relevant outputs from the
registered source language \(\Lang\). The present response therefore cannot
claim source-internal necessity. It can only propose explicit selection laws
whose future audits can decide whether those laws remain source-side and
benchmark-independent.

Let
\[
\Sel=(S_0,S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_F)
\]
denote the selection-law packet. Each \(S_i\) is a rule from registered
source records and declared extension data to a finite object or family of
objects. A selector is admissible only if it is:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item source-defined before benchmark comparison;
    \item natural under source relabeling and monotone order
    reparameterization;
    \item stable under declared refinements or else reports its instability;
    \item blind to target metric, target null-cone, target coordinate, target
    clock, and target rank data; and
    \item failure-tolerant, meaning ambiguity is preserved as a negative or
    underselection result.
\end{enumerate}

\section{S0: Presentation Selector}

\begin{selectionlaw}[Source-accessible finite presentation]
\(S_0\) assigns a finite or locally finite presentation
\[
(\Sub_U,\preceq_U,\Phi_U,\rho_U)
\]
only from a bounded source-accessibility seed, the intrinsic source order, the
registered flow labels, and a declared refinement policy \(\rho_U\). The
selector may close the seed under source-order and source-flow support. It may
not choose \(U\), local finiteness scale, or refinement level because the
choice resembles a target neighborhood.
\end{selectionlaw}

\begin{obligation}[Presentation naturality]
For every admissible source relabeling \(h:\Sub_U\to\Sub_{U'}\), \(S_0\)
must satisfy
\[
h(S_0(U))=S_0(h(U))
\]
up to the declared presentation redundancy. If two allowed refinement
policies produce different adjacency fronts or response ranks, both outcomes
must be carried forward as a selection ambiguity.
\end{obligation}

\section{S1: Transition-Germ Selector}

\begin{selectionlaw}[Minimal source-support transition germ]
\(S_1\) assigns \(\Step\) by a minimal source-support rule: \(x\Step y\) is
admissible only when \(x\) and \(y\) are order-comparable within the
presentation, share declared source-flow support, and no intermediate source
record with the same support class resolves the transition into smaller
source steps. The rule selects a transition family when minimality is
non-unique.
\end{selectionlaw}

\begin{obligation}[Adjacency front invariance]
Neighborhoods \(N_n(x)\), boundaries \(\partial_{\mathcal{S}}R\), and
adjacency fronts derived from \(\Step\) are usable only when they are
invariant over the transition family selected by \(S_1\). If invariance
fails, the packet must report \(\Fail_{\Step}\) with the disagreeing fronts.
\end{obligation}

\section{S2: Perturbation and Discriminator Selector}

\begin{selectionlaw}[Pre-benchmark perturbation and discriminator families]
\(S_2\) assigns perturbation classes \(\mathcal{P}\) and discriminator
families \(\mathcal{D}\) from finite source-local edits, recurrence words,
transition-count windows, and chain-internal response distinctions. Every
threshold, window, and response distinction must be fixed before benchmark
comparison. If multiple source-defined families satisfy the rule, the output
is the family of all such choices, not a hand-selected member.
\end{selectionlaw}

\begin{obligation}[First-response stability]
First-response ranks \(r_D(a,b)\) may support later localization only if they
are stable over every \(D\in\mathcal{D}\) allowed by \(S_2\). If allowed
discriminators disagree, the result is a discriminator underselection report.
\end{obligation}

\section{S3: Response-Algebra Selector}

\begin{selectionlaw}[Quotient by declared source symmetries only]
\(S_3\) assigns \(\RespAlg\) by quotienting response words only by source
relabeling symmetries, recurrence equivalences, and source-local
recombination rules declared before benchmark comparison. The selector does
not impose \(\Rank=4\). It reports the rank spectrum across all admissible
quotients when the quotient rule is not unique.
\end{selectionlaw}

\begin{obligation}[Rank as survey output]
A later candidate may use a rank value only if \(S_3\) makes that value
invariant across admissible quotient choices. A rank-four output is not
privileged. Lower rank, higher rank, or variable rank is a valid negative
control result.
\end{obligation}

\section{S4: Clock-Transport Selector}

\begin{selectionlaw}[Shared-support clock transport]
\(S_4\) assigns stable cycle classes \([\Cycle]\) and transport maps
\[
T_{\Gamma\to\Gamma'}:[\Cycle]_{\Gamma}\to[\Cycle]_{\Gamma'}
\]
only from shared source transition support and recurrence-ratio records. The
transport rule must report holonomy around closed source loops. It may not
normalize holonomy away to match target proper-time behavior.
\end{selectionlaw}

\begin{obligation}[Holonomy transparency]
Clock-cycle comparisons are usable only when transported cycle ratios are
stable over the admissible shared-support relation. Nontrivial holonomy is
reported as source data. Ambiguous transport is reported as a family of
transport outcomes, not collapsed into a single target-compatible clock.
\end{obligation}

\section{S_F: Failure-Reporting Selector}

\begin{failure}[Underselection ledger]
For each selector \(S_i\), the AgentJob using this packet must produce an
underselection ledger with one of the following statuses:
\[
\mathsf{invariant},\quad
\mathsf{family\_valued},\quad
\mathsf{underdetermined},\quad
\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive},\quad
\mathsf{not\_computed}.
\]
Only \(\mathsf{invariant}\) status may support a later candidate object. The
other statuses are carried forward as blockers or negative results.
\end{failure}

\begin{failure}[Benchmark-blind audit record]
Every selector must record the source inputs used, the target inputs not
used, and the time at which benchmark comparison becomes allowed. A selector
whose definition is revised after benchmark comparison is automatically
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\).
\end{failure}

\begin{failure}[No silent repair]
If a selector fails, the packet may recommend repair or refutation. It may
not silently choose a target-compatible branch. It may not authorize
Candidate Constructor or Gate Chair review while selector failure remains
unresolved.
\end{failure}

\section{Control Status}

\begin{status}[Selection laws remain extension hypotheses]
The selection laws above are source-side proposals, not established
consequences of \(\Lang\). They improve the control state by converting
presentation, transition, discriminator, quotient, and transport freedom into
explicit testable rules. They do not prove source-internal necessity and do
not remove the current block on candidate reconstruction.
\end{status}

\begin{verdict}[Draft packet only]
This packet is suitable for audit and refutation. It is not suitable for
candidate construction until a later Smuggling Auditor confirms that the
selectors do not hide target imports and a later Refuter or equivalent
invariance test confirms that the selected outputs are benchmark-independent.
\end{verdict}

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Smuggling audit of selection laws]
The logical next role is a Smuggling Auditor review of \(\Sel\). The audit
should test whether \(S_0\)--\(S_4\) encode target topology, target observer
protocol, target rank, target clock normalization, or benchmark-tuned branch
selection. Candidate Constructor, canonical ontology edit, and Gate Chair
review remain premature.
\end{nextburden}
