\begin{abstract}
This draft/control packet responds to the finite-variation
benchmark-independence refutation by replacing broad benchmark-blindness with
narrow source-forcing obligations. It states what must be proved before the
selector-domain finite-variation layer can support candidate reconstruction:
source-forced ledger provenance, horizon universality, a source-side
admissibility meta-grammar, certificate-invariance theorems, an
adverse-retaining output-basis generator, and \(D_F\) status-transition
theorem obligations. The packet is constructive but non-promotional. It does
not discharge the obligations, edit canonical ontology, authorize candidate
reconstruction, issue a Gate Chair verdict, promote benchmark status, or
claim recovery of an effective relativistic sector. If the obligations cannot
be stated or later discharged without authority expansion or target
selection, the correct status is a controlled pause.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Source-Forcing Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260613-004}.
It inherits the negative result of the finite-variation benchmark-
independence refutation: replayable and target-blind choice surfaces are not
enough. The next burden is to decide whether each choice surface is forced by
source-side structure or must be preserved as an explicit negative-control
family.

Let \(\Lang\) denote the registered source language before the A0--A4
extension sequence. Let
\[
(\LedgerFam,\PackFam,\EnumFam,\CertFam,\OutFam,D_F)
\]
denote the ledger, packet-family, enumerator-family, certificate, output-basis,
and status-transition surfaces named by the prior repair, audit, and
refutation sequence.

\begin{definitionblock}[Source-forcing]
A choice surface \(X\) is source-forced only when there is a source-side rule
\[
\Force_X:\Lang \longrightarrow X
\]
and a proof obligation showing that every \(\Ad\)-admissible and
\(\Blind\)-blind alternative \(X'\) is either equivalent to
\(\Force_X(\Lang)\) up to declared source relabeling, impossible inside the
registered source vocabulary, or retained as a negative-control family whose
outputs and statuses cannot be projected into candidate support.
\end{definitionblock}

\begin{criterion}[No favorable singleton by selection]
An invariant-looking singleton is not admissible candidate support when it is
obtained by choosing a ledger, horizon, grammar, certificate rule, output
basis, or status threshold from a larger source-admissible non-singleton
family. The full family must either collapse by source theorem or remain
recorded as \(\mathsf{family\_valued}\), \(\mathsf{underdetermined}\),
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\), or \(\mathsf{not\_computed}\).
\end{criterion}

\section{Ledger-Provenance Obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Ledger provenance theorem]
The input ledger \(H\) must be generated from a provenance object
\[
\Pi_H=(\mathcal{R}_{0},\mathcal{C}_{0},\mathcal{O}_{0},
\mathcal{Q}_{0},\mathcal{B}_{0})
\]
containing only registered source records, source closure rules, source
operation seeds, source quotient seeds, and pre-benchmark blinding records.
A ledger-provenance theorem must prove one of the following:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(\Pi_H\) determines \(H\) uniquely up to source relabeling;
    \item every admissible \(H'\) generated by the same provenance class is
    enumerated in \(\LedgerFam\); or
    \item the ledger surface is unresolved and all dependent outputs are
    marked \(\mathsf{underdetermined}\) or \(\mathsf{not\_computed}\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{obligation}

\begin{criterion}[Forbidden ledger evidence]
The theorem may not use target metric behavior, target locality, target
observer protocols, target proper-time normalization, target-favorable rank,
or benchmark-success predicates as evidence that \(H\) is the correct ledger.
Blinding timestamps and hashes are provenance evidence only; they do not
prove source necessity.
\end{criterion}

\section{Horizon-Universality Obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Universal horizon family]
The finite horizons \(B_{\min}\), \(B_{\max}\), and every closure-depth rule
must be replaced by a horizon family
\[
\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{src}}=\{B_\eta:\eta\in E\}
\]
generated from source counts, source-order depth, or source saturation rules.
The packet must state a horizon-universality theorem proving that every
admissible horizon either appears in \(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{src}}\), is
excluded by a source proof independent of target behavior, or is recorded as
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\).
\end{obligation}

\begin{criterion}[Monotone saturation check]
If a horizon is justified by saturation, the source-side saturation predicate
must be monotone under admissible source extension. If increasing the horizon
can create an adverse boundary, front, rank, spectrum, transport, or status
row, the smaller horizon cannot support candidate reconstruction.
\end{criterion}

\section{Admissibility Meta-Grammar Obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Meta-grammar for enumerator families]
For each selector-domain family \(D_i\), \(i\in\{0,1,2,3,4\}\), the
enumerator family \(\EnumFam_i\) must be generated by a source-side
meta-grammar
\[
\MetaG_i=(\Sigma_i,\mathcal{R}_i,\mathcal{E}_i,\mathcal{Q}_i,
\mathcal{A}_i),
\]
where \(\Sigma_i\) is a source symbol set, \(\mathcal{R}_i\) is a rewrite or
closure system, \(\mathcal{E}_i\) is an expansion rule, \(\mathcal{Q}_i\) is
a duplicate quotient, and \(\mathcal{A}_i\) is an adverse-output retention
rule. The meta-grammar must generate narrower, broader, and incomparable
source-admissible grammars when those grammars are produced from the same
source seeds.
\end{obligation}

\begin{criterion}[Omission ledger]
Every omitted grammar must receive one and only one source-side label:
\[
\mathsf{duplicate},\quad
\mathsf{out\_of\_horizon},\quad
\mathsf{source\_invalid},\quad
\mathsf{authority\_expansion},\quad
\mathsf{not\_computed}.
\]
The labels ``irrelevant to localization'' and ``unhelpful for the candidate''
are not source-side omission proofs unless a separate source theorem proves
irrelevance across every allowed output basis.
\end{criterion}

\section{Certificate-Invariance Obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Kept/omitted invariance theorem]
For every domain \(D_i\), certificate family \(\CertFam_i\), and object
\(x\), the packet must state a theorem of one of two forms:
\[
\forall C,C'\in\CertFam_i,\quad C(x)=C'(x)
\]
up to declared source relabeling, or
\[
C(x)\neq C'(x)\Rightarrow
\Neg_i(x,C,C')
\]
where \(\Neg_i\) records the resulting certificate-sensitive output as a
negative-control or underdetermined family rather than excluding it.
\end{obligation}

\begin{criterion}[Replay is not invariance]
Replayability proves that a certificate follows a sealed rule. It does not
prove that the rule is source-forced. A kept/omitted decision becomes
candidate-usable only after the certificate-invariance theorem covers every
admissible certificate rule in the declared family.
\end{criterion}

\section{Output-Basis Generator Obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Adverse-retaining output generator]
For each \(D_i\), the output-basis family \(\OutFam_i\) must be generated by
a source-side rule
\[
\mathsf{Gen}_{O,i}:\Lang\times \EnumFam_i\times\CertFam_i
\longrightarrow \OutFam_i
\]
that records favorable, adverse, and neutral source quantities at the same
grain. It must include presentation boundaries, support fronts,
discriminator ranks, quotient spectra, transport cycles, holonomy reports,
and status rows whenever those quantities are source-definable inside the
declared family.
\end{obligation}

\begin{criterion}[Projection blocker]
No output may be removed because it makes the family non-singleton. A
projection to a favorable basis can be reported as a projection, but it
cannot be promoted to source-basis support while a broader source-admissible
basis remains non-invariant.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_F\)}{DF}: Status-Transition Theorem Obligations}

The status vocabulary remains
\[
\mathsf{invariant},\quad
\mathsf{family\_valued},\quad
\mathsf{underdetermined},\quad
\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive},\quad
\mathsf{not\_computed}.
\]

\begin{obligation}[Invariant-transition theorem]
Every transition into \(\mathsf{invariant}\) must cite theorem evidence for:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item ledger provenance uniqueness or complete negative-control coverage;
    \item horizon universality or unresolved-horizon preservation;
    \item meta-grammar completeness for the relevant \(D_i\);
    \item certificate-invariance for kept and omitted cases;
    \item output-basis generator completeness with adverse retention; and
    \item an independent target-import audit of the evidence chain.
\end{enumerate}
\end{obligation}

\begin{obligation}[Status monotonicity]
If any required theorem is absent, the status may move from
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) to \(\mathsf{underdetermined}\) or
\(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) when evidence is added, but it may not move to
\(\mathsf{invariant}\). If target selection or authority expansion is
required to state a theorem, the transition must be blocked.
\end{obligation}

\section{Controlled-Pause Conditions}

\begin{pauserule}[Pause rather than reconstruction]
The Director should preserve a controlled pause if any of the following is
true:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item ledger provenance cannot be source-forced and admissible ledgers
    cannot be fully retained as negative controls;
    \item horizon universality requires a target scale or target locality
    argument;
    \item the admissibility meta-grammar excludes source-definable adverse
    grammars by relevance assertion rather than source theorem;
    \item certificate invariance cannot cover kept and omitted cases;
    \item output-basis generation depends on target observables or favorable
    projection; or
    \item \(D_F\) invariant status requires benchmark success as evidence.
\end{enumerate}
\end{pauserule}

Controlled pause is not global rejection of the \TheoryProgram{}. It is a
local claim-boundary status: the present source-basis path has not yet earned
candidate reconstruction.

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{verdict}[Source-forcing obligation packet specified]
This packet states a narrower source-forcing repair burden. It does not prove
that the ledger, horizons, grammars, certificates, output bases, or \(D_F\)
status transitions are source-forced. It makes the next audit exact: a later
role can test whether these obligations smuggle target structure, require
authority expansion, or remain implementable as source-side theorems.
\end{verdict}

The result is local and non-promotional. Candidate reconstruction, canonical
ontology edit, benchmark promotion, and Gate Chair review remain blocked.
