\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Benchmark-Independence Refutation}\\[0.35em]
\large Source-Internal Necessity Test for A1--A4}
\author{Refuter AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-012-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft tests whether the A1--A4 source-basis axiom packet is
source-internally necessary and benchmark-independent. The result is local
and negative. The packet honestly marks A1--A4 as ontology-extension
hypotheses and avoids direct import of a target metric, null cone, proper
time, or fixed rank four. However, as stated, the axioms do not force unique
localization-relevant outputs from the registered source reservoir. Different
admissible finite presentations, transition germs, discriminator families,
response-algebra quotients, and clock-transport holonomy classes can agree on
the prior source language while disagreeing on fronts, ranks, and transported
cycle comparisons. Candidate reconstruction and Gate Chair review therefore
remain blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Refutation Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260608-012}. It
does not edit canonical ontology TeX, adopt A1--A4 as canonical ontology,
authorize candidate reconstruction, issue a Gate Chair verdict, or reject the
broader \TheoryProgram{}.

Let \(\Lang\) denote the registered source language before the A1--A4
extension packet. Let
\[
\Ext=(\Step,\mathcal{P},\mathcal{D},\{\RespAlg\}_{\Gamma},\mathcal{K})
\]
denote the extension data proposed for localization tests after a finite or
locally finite source presentation has been chosen.

\begin{test}[Source-internal necessity and benchmark independence]
A1--A4 pass this local test only if, for every admissible source presentation
that agrees on the registered \(\Lang\) content, the localization-relevant
outputs used by a later candidate are invariant before benchmark comparison.
The relevant outputs include adjacency fronts, first-response ranks,
response-mode counts, and transported clock-cycle comparisons. If admissible
choices can disagree on those outputs without violating \(\Lang\), then the
axioms are not source-internally necessary as presently stated.
\end{test}

This is not a full no-go theorem. It is a control-level refutation standard:
candidate reconstruction may not proceed from a packet whose decisive
localization outputs can still be selected by admissible extension choices.

\section{A0 and A1: Presentation and Transition Variance}

\begin{finding}[Finite presentation is not source-unique]
A0 introduces a finite or locally finite representation
\((\Sub_U,\preceq_U,\Phi_U)\). The source reservoir constrains order and
source accessibility, but it does not yet determine a unique domain boundary,
coarse-graining scale, or local finiteness convention. These choices can
change adjacency and boundary facts before any target benchmark is consulted.
\end{finding}

\begin{countermodel}[Two source-compatible presentations]
Take the same ordered source records and the same relabeling class. One
presentation treats a bounded interval as a chain of adjacent stages. Another
presentation refines each stage into two source-local records with identical
order projection but different local edge incidence. Both can preserve the
registered source order and source-flow labeling. They need not preserve the
same \(N_n(x)\), boundary \(\partial_{\mathcal{S}}R\), or propagation front.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{finding}[Transition germ is not forced by order compatibility]
A1 requires \(\Step\) to respect source order, locality, relabeling
invariance, and no benchmark fitting. Those are necessary anti-smuggling
conditions. They are not sufficient to select the edge relation. Multiple
edge sets can satisfy them while producing different adjacency neighborhoods.
\end{finding}

\begin{blocker}[Presentation-invariance blocker]
A1 is not benchmark-independent until either a source law fixes the
presentation and transition germ, or a theorem shows that localization outputs
are invariant across all admissible choices. Without that, target-like
neighborhoods can be selected while remaining source-named.
\end{blocker}

\section{A2: Perturbation and Discriminator Variance}

\begin{finding}[Pre-benchmark fixation is not uniqueness]
A2 correctly requires perturbations and discriminators to be fixed before
benchmark comparison. But pre-benchmark fixation alone does not prove
source-internal necessity. The registered source language does not yet choose
which recurrence words, transition-count windows, or response distinctions
are admissible.
\end{finding}

\begin{countermodel}[Discriminator divergence]
For the same \(\Step\)-presentation, define one discriminator to fire at the
first changed recurrence word and another to fire only after a stable
two-cycle response distinction. Both use source records and can be fixed
before benchmark comparison. They can still assign different first-response
ranks \(r_D(a,b)\), generating different fronts from the same \(\Lang\)
content.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Discriminator-invariance blocker]
A2 remains an auditable extension hypothesis, not a source-internal
necessity result. A candidate may use it only if a source rule selects
\(\mathcal{P}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\), or if non-unique discriminator outcomes
are reported as negative results rather than tuned away.
\end{blocker}

\section{A3: Response-Algebra and Rank Variance}

\begin{finding}[Avoiding fixed rank four does not fix the quotient]
A3 improves the control state by refusing to impose \(\Rank=4\). The
remaining problem is the quotient rule. The ground response-word set,
relabeling symmetries, recombination operations, and independence criterion
are not yet forced by \(\Lang\).
\end{finding}

\begin{countermodel}[Quotient variation]
Two source-compatible response algebras may use the same observer-chain
records. One quotient identifies two response functionals because their
recurrence words agree under a chosen recombination rule. Another keeps them
independent because the relevant response supports differ locally. The first
can report lower rank while the second reports higher rank. Neither outcome
is source-internally necessary until the quotient rule is fixed by source
dynamics.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Rank-invariance blocker]
A3 is benchmark-independent only if the response algebra is source-selected
or if every admissible quotient is surveyed and the rank is treated as an
empirical source output. A later four-mode localization candidate may not
select the quotient that yields the target count.
\end{blocker}

\section{A4: Clock-Transport and Holonomy Variance}

\begin{finding}[Holonomy reporting is necessary but not determinative]
A4 requires nontrivial holonomy to be reported rather than normalized away.
That is a necessary anti-smuggling condition. It does not determine the
shared-support relation, transport map, or equivalence class of stable cycles.
\end{finding}

\begin{countermodel}[Transport divergence]
Fix the same source chains and the same local cycle records. One admissible
transport rule may compare cycles only across shared transition support.
Another may allow a wider overlap relation between adjacent chains. Both can
report holonomy honestly, but they may produce different transported clock
ratios around the same loop. The difference is source-relevant, not a target
metric result, yet it blocks unique candidate reconstruction.
\end{countermodel}

\begin{blocker}[Clock-transport blocker]
A4 is not yet source-internally necessary. It becomes usable for candidate
construction only after the source dynamics fix transport, or after a
failure-tolerant holonomy survey shows that the candidate's clock-relevant
claims are invariant under admissible transport choices.
\end{blocker}

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{verdict}[A1--A4 do not yet pass benchmark-independence refutation]
A1--A4 are valuable because they expose the exact extension hypotheses needed
for a localization program. They do not yet pass the stronger test of
source-internal necessity and benchmark independence. The packet still allows
admissible source-named choices that alter localization-relevant outputs
before benchmark comparison. Therefore, the present state supports only a
negative control result: A1--A4 may remain explicit extension hypotheses to
audit, but they do not authorize candidate reconstruction.
\end{verdict}

This is a local result. It does not reject the \TheoryProgram{} and does not
decide whether future source dynamics could force a unique transition,
response, rank, or transport structure.

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Source-internal selection law or controlled pause]
The logical next step is not Candidate Constructor and not Gate Chair review.
The next bounded task should either:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item route to an Ontology Formalizer to propose a source-internal
    selection-law packet for A0--A4, including explicit invariance obligations
    and failure-reporting rules; or
    \item enter a Director-controlled pause if adding those laws exceeds the
    current ontology-extension authority.
\end{enumerate}
\end{nextburden}

Any future candidate must first show that its localization outputs survive
admissible variation in presentation, transition, discriminator, quotient,
and transport choices.
