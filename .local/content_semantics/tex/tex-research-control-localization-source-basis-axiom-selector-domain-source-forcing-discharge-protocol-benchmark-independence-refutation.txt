\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Selector-Domain Source-Forcing Discharge Protocol Benchmark-Independence Refutation}\\[0.35em]
\large Stability Test for Four-Cell Ledger Classification Under Source-Side Variation}
\author{Refuter AgentJob AJ-RT-20260613-008-001}
\date{June 13, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft/control artifact stress-tests the audited source-forcing
discharge protocol for benchmark independence. The protocol is a useful
control improvement: it separates current-language theorem burdens,
negative-control families, protected authority-expansion triggers, and
unresolved statuses for ledger provenance, horizon universality,
admissibility meta-grammar, certificate invariance, output-basis generation,
and \(D_F\) status transitions. The stress test result is local and
negative. The four-cell ledger is not yet benchmark-independent because
admissible source-side variation can change which cells receive theorem
burdens, negative controls, expansion triggers, or unresolved statuses. The
protocol remains a disciplined pause ledger rather than candidate-support
evidence. Candidate reconstruction, canonical ontology edit, benchmark
promotion, Gate Chair review, and effective-sector recovery claims remain
blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Refutation Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260613-008}.
It does not edit canonical ontology TeX, adopt A0--A4 or selection laws as
canonical ontology, authorize candidate reconstruction, issue a Gate Chair
verdict, promote benchmark status, or reject the broader \TheoryProgram{}.

The audited protocol assigns, for each surface
\[
X\in\{H,\HorizonFam,\EnumFam_i,\CertFam_i,\OutFam_i,D_F\},
\]
a four-cell discharge record
\[
\mathcal{D}_X=(\Current_X,\Neg_X,\Expand_X,\Unres_X).
\]
The smuggling audit found that this record is target-clean as a separation
ledger. The present Refuter test asks a different question: whether the cell
assignment remains stable under admissible source-side variation.

\begin{test}[Four-cell benchmark independence]
Let \(\mathcal{D}\) and \(\mathcal{D}'\) be discharge ledgers produced from
two \(\Ad\)-admissible and \(\Blind\)-blind source-side choices over the same
registered source vocabulary. The protocol is benchmark-independent only if
each surface preserves the same candidate-relevant status up to declared
source relabeling, or if every disagreement is explicitly retained as a
negative-control or unresolved family that cannot support candidate
reconstruction.
\end{test}

The fact that a cell assignment is target-clean does not make it
source-forced. Benchmark independence requires stability under admissible
ledger, horizon, grammar, certificate, output-basis, and status-rule
variation.

\section{Ledger-Provenance Variation}

\begin{finding}[The ledger cell assignment is not source-forced]
The protocol correctly routes target-selected seeds and new provenance
authority to \(\Expand_H\). It still does not prove that the provenance tuple
\[
\Pi_H=(\mathcal{R}_{0},\mathcal{C}_{0},\mathcal{O}_{0},
\mathcal{Q}_{0},\mathcal{B}_{0})
\]
is unique up to source relabeling or that every admissible ledger family has
been completely retained.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Competing source ledgers]
Two ledgers can use only registered records, source closure rules, source
operation seeds, source quotient seeds, and pre-benchmark blinding records
while differing in closure breadth or quotient refinement. One ledger may
place provenance uniqueness into \(\Current_H\). Another may reveal an
incomparable admissible family that belongs in \(\Neg_H\), or may expose a
missing source rule that belongs in \(\Expand_H\), or may leave dependent
rows in \(\Unres_H\).
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Ledger classification instability]
Because the protocol has not supplied a source theorem or complete
admissible ledger inventory, the assignment of \(H\) to current burden,
negative control, expansion trigger, or unresolved status can change under
admissible source-side variation. This blocks benchmark-independent use of
the ledger cell.
\end{blocker}

\section{Horizon-Universality Variation}

\begin{finding}[Horizon universality remains a claim-bearing choice]
The protocol requires a source-generated horizon family
\[
\HorizonFam=\{B_\eta:\eta\in E\}
\]
and monotone saturation evidence. That requirement is correct. It is not yet
a theorem that all admissible horizons are included, source-impossible, or
retained as unresolved.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Horizon families with different adverse exposure]
One admissible horizon rule may saturate early and expose no adverse
presentation boundary or transport cycle. A broader or incomparable
source-side horizon rule may generate additional boundaries, spectra,
cycles, or status rows. Both rules can be benchmark-blind and target-clean.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Horizon cell instability]
Until horizon universality is proved, a favorable horizon can be recorded as
\(\Current_{\HorizonFam}\) in one ledger while a broader admissible horizon
belongs in \(\Neg_{\HorizonFam}\) or \(\Unres_{\HorizonFam}\). The protocol
therefore does not yet provide benchmark-independent candidate support.
\end{blocker}

\section{Admissibility Meta-Grammar Variation}

\begin{finding}[Meta-grammar completeness is not established]
The protocol names the necessary source-side meta-grammar
\[
\MetaG_i=(\Sigma_i,\mathcal{R}_i,\mathcal{E}_i,\mathcal{Q}_i,\mathcal{A}_i)
\]
for each \(D_i\). It does not prove that the grammar family is complete over
narrower, broader, and incomparable source-admissible grammars.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Omission labels can move between cells]
An omitted grammar may be labeled \(\mathsf{duplicate}\) under one quotient,
\(\mathsf{source\_invalid}\) under a narrower source syntax,
\(\mathsf{authority\_expansion}\) under a broader syntax, or
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) when no source theorem decides the case. These
labels can remain target-clean while changing whether the grammar appears in
\(\Current_{\EnumFam_i}\), \(\Neg_{\EnumFam_i}\),
\(\Expand_{\EnumFam_i}\), or \(\Unres_{\EnumFam_i}\).
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Grammar classification instability]
The current protocol cannot prove that adverse grammars are all generated,
classified, and retained under a source-forced meta-grammar. Therefore the
grammar cell assignment is not benchmark-independent.
\end{blocker}

\section{Certificate-Invariance Variation}

\begin{finding}[Certificate replay is not certificate invariance]
The protocol correctly separates replayable certificates from invariance
theorems. It does not yet prove that kept and omitted cases agree across
every admissible certificate rule or that every disagreement is preserved as
certificate-sensitive negative control.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Kept and omitted disagreements]
One certificate rule can omit a case as duplicate or out of horizon. Another
admissible rule can retain the same case because it changes a boundary,
rank, quotient spectrum, transport cycle, holonomy report, or status row.
Both rules can be sealed and replayable.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Certificate cell instability]
Without a certificate-invariance theorem, certificate disagreements may
move a case between \(\Current_{\CertFam_i}\), \(\Neg_{\CertFam_i}\),
\(\Expand_{\CertFam_i}\), and \(\Unres_{\CertFam_i}\). Replayability of a
selected rule does not discharge benchmark independence.
\end{blocker}

\section{Output-Basis Variation}

\begin{finding}[Output-basis generation is not yet source-forced]
The protocol requires an adverse-retaining source-side generator
\[
\mathsf{Gen}_{O,i}:\Lang\times\EnumFam_i\times\CertFam_i\to\OutFam_i .
\]
It does not prove that the output vocabulary and grain are already
source-definable in \(\Lang\), nor that broader admissible output bases are
irrelevant by source theorem.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Projection versus adverse-retaining basis]
A narrow output basis may report a singleton presentation boundary or rank.
A broader source-admissible basis may also include quotient spectra,
transport cycles, holonomy reports, or status rows that are non-singleton.
If the narrow basis appears invariant while the broad basis remains
family-valued, the favorable result is a projection artifact.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Output cell instability]
The protocol blocks favorable projection as a rule, but it does not yet
force the output-basis generator. Different target-clean output bases can
move evidence between \(\Current_{\OutFam_i}\), \(\Neg_{\OutFam_i}\),
\(\Expand_{\OutFam_i}\), and \(\Unres_{\OutFam_i}\). Candidate support is
therefore blocked.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_F\)}{DF} Status-Rule Variation}

\begin{finding}[Invariant status still requires a transition theorem]
The protocol correctly blocks transitions into \(\mathsf{invariant}\) when
ledger provenance, horizon universality, meta-grammar completeness,
certificate invariance, output-basis generator completeness, or independent
target-import audit is absent. It has not proved that these prerequisites
are themselves stable under admissible source-side variation.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Status rules with different thresholds]
One source-side status rule may keep a row
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) until every surface has theorem evidence. Another
may move the same row to \(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) after broader negative
controls are enumerated. A third may require protected authority to define a
permanent transition rule. These differences change the \(D_F\) ledger
without importing target metrics or target clocks.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Status cell instability]
Because \(D_F\) transition rules are not source-forced, the same evidence
can produce different non-promotional statuses across admissible rules. The
safe classification is controlled pause, not invariant candidate support.
\end{blocker}

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{verdict}[Protocol fails the current benchmark-independence test]
The source-forcing discharge protocol passes a useful target-import and
authority-separation audit, but it does not yet establish benchmark
independence. Admissible source-side variation can change which four-cell
ledger entries receive current-language theorem burdens, negative controls,
protected expansion triggers, or unresolved statuses. The protocol therefore
remains a controlled-pause ledger rather than candidate-support evidence.
\end{verdict}

This is a local negative result. It does not reject the \TheoryProgram{} and
does not reject source-forcing theorem work as a research path. It blocks
candidate reconstruction from the present audited protocol.

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Single-surface theorem attempt or controlled pause]
The logical next step is not Candidate Constructor and not Gate Chair review.
The next bounded task should route to an Ontology Formalizer only if the
Director determines that one surface can receive a narrower current-language
theorem attempt without authority expansion. The least broad target is
ledger provenance: either prove that \(\Pi_H\) is generated and unique up to
source relabeling inside \(\Lang\), or preserve a controlled pause with all
admissible ledger alternatives retained as negative controls. If no
single-surface theorem attempt is feasible without protected expansion, the
Director should record a controlled pause rather than authorize candidate
reconstruction.
\end{nextburden}
