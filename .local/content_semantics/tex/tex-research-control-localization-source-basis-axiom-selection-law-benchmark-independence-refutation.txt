\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Selection-Law Benchmark-Independence Refutation}\\[0.35em]
\large Invariance Test for Source-Internal Selector Rules}
\author{Refuter AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-015-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft tests whether the selection-law packet
\(\Sel=(S_0,S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_F)\) is benchmark-independent over its
admissible source-defined selector classes. The result is local and negative.
The packet improves the control state by making selectors explicit, requiring
benchmark blindness, and preserving failure states. However, as stated, it
does not yet define survey domains tightly enough to prove invariant
localization-relevant outputs. Admissible seeds, refinements, support
equivalences, discriminator families, response quotients, and clock
transports can agree on the prior source language while disagreeing on fronts,
first-response ranks, rank spectra, transported cycle ratios, or failure
statuses. Candidate reconstruction and Gate Chair review remain blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Refutation Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260608-015}. It
does not edit canonical ontology TeX, adopt A0--A4 or \(S_0\)--\(S_F\) as
canonical ontology, authorize candidate reconstruction, issue a Gate Chair
verdict, or reject the broader \TheoryProgram{}.

Let \(\Lang\) denote the registered source language before the A0--A4
extension sequence. Let \(\Sel\) denote the source-internal selection-law
packet proposed after the earlier benchmark-independence refutation of A1--A4.

\begin{test}[Selector benchmark independence]
\(\Sel\) passes this local test only if every localization-relevant output
available to a later candidate is invariant across the admissible
source-defined class for the corresponding selector. The required outputs
include adjacency fronts, first-response ranks, response-rank spectra,
transported cycle ratios, holonomy reports, and failure-ledger status.
\end{test}

Benchmark independence is stronger than pre-benchmark declaration. A selector
can be fixed before benchmark comparison and still fail this test if the
admissible source-defined class permits multiple non-equivalent outputs.

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_0\)}{S0}: Seed and Refinement Variation}

\begin{finding}[The presentation survey domain is not fixed]
\(S_0\) requires a source-accessible seed and a declared refinement policy.
This is an improvement over implicit presentation choice. It does not by
itself define the complete class of admissible seeds, closure radii, or
refinement policies that must be surveyed before a candidate can use the
result.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Refinement-disagreement model]
Take the same source order and flow labels. One admissible policy closes a
bounded seed under immediate source-flow support. Another closes under two
source-flow steps and then refines records that share support labels. Both
are source-named and can be chosen before benchmark comparison. They can
produce different boundary records, adjacency front widths, and response-rank
inputs.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Presentation-invariance failure]
\(S_0\) is not benchmark-independent until the source dynamics fix the seed
and refinement policy or the packet proves that all admissible policies yield
the same localization-relevant outputs. The current packet requires ambiguity
to be reported, but it does not yet perform or define the complete survey.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_1\)}{S1}: Support-Equivalence Variation}

\begin{finding}[Minimal support depends on support classification]
\(S_1\) selects \(\Step\) by order comparability, shared support, and absence
of an intermediate record with the same support class. The benchmark
independence burden therefore moves to the support-class rule. The packet
does not yet prove that all source-admissible support classifications induce
the same transition family.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Transition-front divergence]
For the same records, classify support by exact recurrence word in one case
and by recurrence-word prefix in another. Both classifications can be
source-defined. The exact rule can split a transition into smaller steps,
while the prefix rule can merge the same records into a direct transition.
The induced neighborhoods \(N_n(x)\), boundaries, and fronts need not agree.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Transition-family invariance failure]
\(S_1\) cannot support candidate reconstruction until support equivalence is
source-fixed or until non-invariant transition families are preserved as a
negative result. Selecting the front family that resembles a benchmark
geometry would be benchmark selection, even if the vocabulary remains
source-side.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_2\)}{S2}: Perturbation and Discriminator Variation}

\begin{finding}[Family-valued output is not positive evidence]
\(S_2\) correctly carries all source-defined perturbation and discriminator
families rather than selecting a single convenient member. But a family-valued
output is not a benchmark-independent localization datum unless all members
agree on the first-response ranks relevant to the later candidate.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[First-response-rank divergence]
One discriminator may fire on the first changed recurrence word. Another may
fire only after a stable two-step response distinction. Both can be fixed
before benchmark comparison and both can use only source records. They can
assign different first-response ranks \(r_D(a,b)\), producing different
fronts or rank ledgers.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Discriminator survey blocker]
The present packet makes the survey obligation visible, but it does not yet
establish stability of \(r_D(a,b)\) across the admissible family. Therefore
the discriminator output remains a blocker unless reported as
\(\mathsf{family\_valued}\), \(\mathsf{underdetermined}\), or
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\).
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_3\)}{S3}: Response-Quotient and Rank Variation}

\begin{finding}[Refusing rank four is necessary but not sufficient]
\(S_3\) avoids imposing \(\Rank=4\). That is a necessary control success. The
remaining benchmark-independence question is whether the response-word ground
set, recurrence equivalence, and recombination quotient are source-unique or
at least rank-invariant.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Rank-spectrum divergence]
Two admissible quotients can agree on source records and disagree on
independence. One quotient identifies response functionals by recurrence
equivalence; another keeps them independent when their local supports differ.
The resulting rank spectrum can vary. A favorable rank value is not evidence
unless the packet surveys the admissible quotient class and records the whole
spectrum.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Rank-invariance failure]
\(S_3\) currently supports at most a rank-survey obligation. It does not
support candidate reconstruction unless the rank is invariant or the
candidate is explicitly formulated to survive the full reported spectrum.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_4\)}{S4}: Transport and Holonomy Variation}

\begin{finding}[Holonomy transparency does not fix transport]
\(S_4\) requires nontrivial holonomy to be reported rather than normalized
away. This prevents one direct form of clock smuggling. It does not determine
the shared-support relation, the transport map, or the stable-cycle
equivalence class.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Transport-ratio divergence]
Using the same source chains, one admissible rule may compare cycles only
when their transition supports coincide exactly. Another may compare cycles
when their support classes overlap through a declared refinement. Both rules
can report holonomy honestly. They can still produce different transported
cycle ratios around the same source loop.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Clock-transport invariance failure]
\(S_4\) is not benchmark-independent until the transport rule is source-fixed
or until transported ratios and holonomy reports are stable over the declared
survey domain. A single target-compatible clock comparison may not be selected
from a non-invariant transport family.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(S_F\)}{SF}: Failure-Ledger Status Variation}

\begin{finding}[Failure reporting blocks silent repair but does not prove invariance]
\(S_F\) is a useful safeguard: it distinguishes \(\mathsf{invariant}\),
\(\mathsf{family\_valued}\), \(\mathsf{underdetermined}\),
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\), and \(\mathsf{not\_computed}\). The local
problem is that status assignment itself depends on a declared survey domain.
Without that domain, \(\mathsf{invariant}\) is not an auditable claim.
\end{finding}

\begin{blocker}[Status-domain blocker]
The failure ledger must identify the class surveyed, the source inputs used,
the benchmark inputs excluded, and the rule for changing status from
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) to \(\mathsf{invariant}\). Otherwise the ledger is
control bookkeeping, not benchmark-independence evidence.
\end{blocker}

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{verdict}[Selection-law packet fails the current benchmark-independence test]
The selection-law packet is a genuine control improvement because it makes
selector choices explicit and forbids silent target-compatible repair. It
does not yet pass benchmark independence. The packet currently exposes the
right selector surfaces but does not prove invariant localization-relevant
outputs across admissible source-defined selector classes. Candidate
reconstruction, canonical ontology adoption, benchmark promotion, and Gate
Chair review therefore remain blocked.
\end{verdict}

This is a local result. It does not reject the \TheoryProgram{} and does not
preclude a future source-dynamics theorem or selector-domain repair packet
from establishing the required invariance.

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Selector-domain repair or controlled pause]
The logical next step is not Candidate Constructor and not Gate Chair review.
The next bounded task should either:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item route to an Ontology Formalizer to propose a selector-domain repair
    packet that fixes the admissible survey classes for \(S_0\)--\(S_F\),
    states invariance criteria, and defines when non-invariance is a negative
    result; or
    \item enter a Director-controlled pause if further selector-domain
    commitments exceed current ontology-extension authority.
\end{enumerate}
\end{nextburden}

Any future candidate must first show that its localization-relevant outputs
survive admissible variation in seed, refinement, support equivalence,
discriminator family, response quotient, transport, and failure-ledger status.
