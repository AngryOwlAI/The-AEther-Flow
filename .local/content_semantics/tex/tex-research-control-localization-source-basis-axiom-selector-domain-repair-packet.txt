\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Selector-Domain Repair Packet}\\[0.35em]
\large Survey Domains, Invariance Criteria, and Negative-Result Status Rules for \(S_0\)--\(S_F\)}
\author{Ontology Formalizer AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-016-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft responds to the local benchmark-independence refutation of the
selection-law packet. It does not promote A0--A4, adopt \(S_0\)--\(S_F\) as
canonical ontology, edit canonical ontology, authorize candidate
reconstruction, or issue a Gate Chair verdict. It proposes a repair layer:
for each selector, define an explicit source-side survey domain and an
invariance criterion. A later candidate may use only outputs whose survey
domain is declared, benchmark-blind, and invariant. Non-invariant,
underdetermined, benchmark-sensitive, or uncomputed outputs remain negative
control results.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Repair Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260608-016}.
It repairs the control form of \(\Sel=(S_0,S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_F)\) by making
the survey domains explicit. It does not prove that the selectors pass those
domains, and it does not claim that source dynamics uniquely determine the
localization-relevant outputs.

Let \(\Lang\) be the registered source language before the A0--A4 extension
sequence. A repaired selector is not a single branch. It is a pair
\[
S_i^{\mathrm{rep}}=(\Dom_i,\Inv_i)
\]
where \(\Dom_i\) is the admissible source-defined survey class and \(\Inv_i\)
is the invariance criterion that decides whether a later role may use the
output.

\begin{ledger}[Common admissibility screen]
Each domain \(\Dom_i\) must be fixed before benchmark comparison, natural
under source relabeling and monotone order reparameterization, blind to
target metric, target null-cone, target coordinate, target clock, and target
rank data, and finite or effectively enumerable from declared source inputs.
If any condition is missing, the selector status is not
\(\mathsf{invariant}\).
\end{ledger}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_0\)}{D0}: Presentation-Domain Repair}

\begin{domainrule}[Seed and refinement survey domain]
\(\Dom_0\) is the set of triples \((U,C,\rho)\), where \(U\) is a bounded
source-accessibility seed, \(C\) is a source-order and source-flow closure
operator, and \(\rho\) is a refinement policy. Membership is allowed only
when \(U\), \(C\), and \(\rho\) are named by source records and declared
before benchmark comparison. The domain must include every such triple within
the declared locality bound, not only a favorable representative.
\end{domainrule}

\begin{criterion}[Presentation invariance]
\(\Inv_0\) holds only when the induced boundaries, adjacency-front inputs,
and response-rank inputs are isomorphic under source relabeling across all
\((U,C,\rho)\in\Dom_0\). If the outputs differ, the ledger records
\(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) with the disagreeing presentations.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_1\)}{D1}: Transition-Support Repair}

\begin{domainrule}[Support-equivalence survey domain]
\(\Dom_1\) is the set of source-defined support-equivalence relations and
minimality tests compatible with the presentation domain. A support
equivalence may use recurrence words, source-flow labels, and order support.
It may not use target topology, target geodesic adjacency, target observer
protocols, or benchmark success.
\end{domainrule}

\begin{criterion}[Transition-front invariance]
\(\Inv_1\) holds only when every \(\Step\) induced by \(\Dom_1\) gives the
same localization-relevant neighborhood and front data up to source
isomorphism. If exact support and coarser support induce different fronts,
the selector status is \(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) or
\(\mathsf{underdetermined}\), not positive evidence.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_2\)}{D2}: Perturbation and Discriminator Repair}

\begin{domainrule}[Closed discriminator-family domain]
\(\Dom_2\) is the closure of all source-local perturbations and
discriminators generated by declared recurrence words, finite transition
windows, chain-internal response distinctions, and source-local edit rules.
Thresholds and windows must be declared before benchmark comparison. Revising
them after benchmark comparison sets the status to
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\).
\end{domainrule}

\begin{criterion}[First-response-rank invariance]
\(\Inv_2\) holds only when first-response ranks \(r_D(a,b)\) are stable over
every \(D\in\Dom_2\) for the source pairs used by a later candidate. If the
ranks vary, the output is a discriminator-family spectrum and cannot be
collapsed into a candidate-supporting rank.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_3\)}{D3}: Response-Quotient Repair}

\begin{domainrule}[Response-algebra quotient domain]
\(\Dom_3\) is the set of response-word ground sets, recurrence equivalences,
source relabeling symmetries, and recombination quotients generated by the
declared source records. No member of \(\Dom_3\) may be selected because it
yields a target-favorable rank. The full quotient class must be reported.
\end{domainrule}

\begin{criterion}[Rank-spectrum invariance]
\(\Inv_3\) holds only when the rank spectrum over \(\Dom_3\) is a singleton
or when a later candidate is formulated to remain valid over the full
reported spectrum. A rank-four member of a non-singleton spectrum has no
special status.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_4\)}{D4}: Clock-Transport Repair}

\begin{domainrule}[Shared-support transport domain]
\(\Dom_4\) is the set of shared-support relations, stable-cycle equivalences,
and transport maps generated from source transition support and recurrence
ratio records. The domain must include exact-support, declared-overlap, and
refinement-compatible transport rules when those rules are source-definable.
It may not encode target synchronization or proper-time normalization.
\end{domainrule}

\begin{criterion}[Transport and holonomy invariance]
\(\Inv_4\) holds only when transported cycle ratios and holonomy reports are
stable over \(\Dom_4\). Nontrivial holonomy is source data. Divergent
transport ratios are negative control outputs unless a later theorem proves
that the divergence is irrelevant to the candidate claim.
\end{criterion}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_F\)}{DF}: Failure-Ledger Repair}

\begin{ledger}[Status metadata]
For each selector, \(S_F^{\mathrm{rep}}\) must record the surveyed domain
\(\Dom_i\), source inputs used, target inputs excluded, benchmark-comparison
time, output family, invariance criterion, and status. The allowed statuses
are
\[
\mathsf{invariant},\quad
\mathsf{family\_valued},\quad
\mathsf{underdetermined},\quad
\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive},\quad
\mathsf{not\_computed}.
\]
\end{ledger}

\begin{ledger}[Status transition rule]
\(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) may become \(\mathsf{invariant}\) only after the
declared domain is surveyed. \(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) may become
\(\mathsf{invariant}\) only after a source-side theorem shows that the
family differences are irrelevant to the candidate output. A
\(\mathsf{benchmark\_sensitive}\) status requires repair or rejection before
candidate reconstruction.
\end{ledger}

\(\Dom_0\) & seeds, closures, refinements & presentation outputs invariant \\
\(\Dom_1\) & support equivalences, minimality tests & transition fronts invariant \\
\(\Dom_2\) & perturbations, discriminators & first-response ranks invariant \\
\(\Dom_3\) & quotients, recombination rules & singleton or claim-stable rank spectrum \\
\(\Dom_4\) & transport maps, cycle equivalences & transported ratios and holonomy stable \\
\(\Dom_F\) & status metadata and transition rules & no unsurveyed invariant claim \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Control Status}

\begin{status}[Repair packet remains an extension hypothesis]
This packet improves the control state by turning the prior underselection
findings into explicit survey-domain obligations. It does not prove that the
obligations are satisfiable. It does not promote the selection laws to
canonical ontology and does not authorize candidate reconstruction.
\end{status}

\begin{verdict}[Suitable for audit and refutation only]
The repaired domains are suitable for a Smuggling Auditor review and later
Refuter test. They are not suitable for Candidate Constructor use unless the
repair layer passes hidden-target-import audit and benchmark-independence
testing.
\end{verdict}

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Audit selector-domain repair]
The logical next role is a Smuggling Auditor AgentJob. It should test whether
\(\Dom_0\)--\(\Dom_F\) hide target locality, topology, observer protocol,
rank, clock normalization, or benchmark-tuned branch selection in their
domain definitions. Candidate Constructor, canonical ontology edit, and Gate
Chair review remain premature.
\end{nextburden}
