\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Smuggling Audit}\\[0.35em]
\large Target-Import Review of A1--A4 Extension Hypotheses}
\author{Smuggling Auditor AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-011-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft audits the minimal source-basis axiom justification packet A1--A4
for hidden target imports. The packet improves the control state by declaring
the transition, perturbation, response-algebra, and clock-transport structures
as explicit ontology-extension hypotheses rather than conservative
consequences of the original source reservoir. That transparency is necessary
but not sufficient for candidate reconstruction. The audit finds no direct
metric primitive, but identifies unresolved target-import channels in finite
presentation choice, transition incidence, discriminator admissibility,
response-rank quotienting, and clock-transport holonomy. Candidate
reconstruction and Gate Chair review remain blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Audit Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task
\texttt{RT-20260608-011}. It does not edit canonical ontology TeX, adopt
A1--A4 as canonical ontology, authorize candidate reconstruction, issue a
Gate Chair verdict, or reject the broader \TheoryProgram{}.

The object under audit is the axiom packet
\[
(\mathrm{A0},\mathrm{A1},\mathrm{A2},\mathrm{A3},\mathrm{A4})
\]
introduced after the local conservativity refutation of the source-basis
packet. The audit standard is twofold:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item whether the packet explicitly imports target geometry, target
    observer protocol, target clock normalization, or target rank; and
    \item whether the packet leaves selection freedom that can silently encode
    those target structures before benchmark comparison.
\end{enumerate}

The second standard is stricter. A source-named axiom can still smuggle target
content if its admissible presentations are chosen because they reproduce the
target.

\section{Positive Transparency Screen}

\begin{auditfinding}[Extension status is stated correctly]
The axiom justification packet does not claim that \(\Step\),
\(\mathcal{P}\), \(\mathcal{D}\), \(\RespAlg\), or \(\mathcal{K}\) are
conservative over the original source language \(\Lang\). It labels them as
explicit ontology-extension hypotheses. This is a material improvement over a
packet that would disguise new structure as a definition.
\end{auditfinding}

\begin{auditfinding}[No direct target metric primitive]
The packet does not declare a Lorentzian metric, target null cone,
radar-coordinate construction, Einstein equation, or target proper-time
normalization as a source primitive.
\end{auditfinding}

These positive findings are control findings only. They do not make A1--A4
promotion-ready. Transparent extension status tells the project where the
burden lies; it does not discharge that burden.

\section{A0 and A1: Presentation and Transition Risks}

\begin{auditfinding}[Finite presentation is a localization selector]
A0 introduces a finite or locally finite source presentation
\((\Sub_U,\preceq_U,\Phi_U)\). This may be a useful control device, but the
choice of \(U\), local finiteness scale, and presentation equivalence class can
select the localization arena. If those choices are made to resemble a target
manifold neighborhood, target topology can enter before A1 is even applied.
\end{auditfinding}

\begin{smugglingrisk}[Transition incidence can encode target neighborhoods]
A1 makes \(\Step\) the primitive edge relation of the presentation and derives
adjacency from it. Order compatibility and relabeling invariance do not
determine the edge set. A benchmark-compatible edge set can reproduce target
causal fronts, spatial balls, or continuum-neighborhood incidence while still
being described in source language.
\end{smugglingrisk}

\begin{promotionblocker}[Topology blocker]
A1 may be used only as an explicit hypothesis to audit. It cannot support
candidate reconstruction until a source-independent rule fixes the finite
presentation and \(\Step\), or until a Refuter shows that localization outputs
are invariant across all admissible presentation and transition choices.
\end{promotionblocker}

\section{A2: Perturbation and Discriminator Risks}

\begin{auditfinding}[A2 names the tuning channel but does not close it]
A2 requires perturbations and discriminators to be fixed by source recurrence
words, transition counts, and response distinctions before benchmark
comparison. This is the right anti-smuggling form. It remains incomplete
because the admissible recurrence words, count windows, and response
distinctions are not uniquely generated by the registered source reservoir.
\end{auditfinding}

\begin{smugglingrisk}[Discrete benchmark tuning]
Different source-named discriminator families can produce different first
detection ranks over the same presentation. If the family is selected after
checking benchmark agreement, A2 becomes a discrete target-fitting channel
rather than a source law.
\end{smugglingrisk}

\begin{promotionblocker}[Admissibility blocker]
Candidate reconstruction remains blocked until \(\mathcal{P}\) and
\(\mathcal{D}\) are fixed by a pre-benchmark source rule, or until the
candidate commits to reporting non-uniqueness whenever admissible choices
disagree.
\end{promotionblocker}

\section{A3: Response-Algebra and Rank Risks}

\begin{auditfinding}[Rank survey is better than rank imposition]
A3 explicitly says that no axiom sets \(\Rank=4\). This avoids the most direct
target \(1+3\) import. The packet instead requires the response algebra to
report whatever rank its source records supply.
\end{auditfinding}

\begin{smugglingrisk}[Quotient freedom can hide rank selection]
The risk moves from the number four to the quotient construction. If the
ground response words, equivalence relation, or independence criterion are
chosen so that a four-rank response domain appears, target rank has been
imported through algebra design rather than asserted directly.
\end{smugglingrisk}

\begin{promotionblocker}[Rank-production blocker]
A3 cannot support candidate reconstruction until the source dynamics fix the
response-word ground set and quotient rule, or until all admissible quotient
choices are surveyed and the result is treated as a possible negative result
when the source rank is not four.
\end{promotionblocker}

\section{A4: Clock-Transport Risks}

\begin{auditfinding}[Holonomy reporting is the correct burden]
A4 states that clock-transport holonomy must be reported as source data rather
than normalized away to match a target clock. This directly addresses the
prior clock-scale smuggling channel.
\end{auditfinding}

\begin{smugglingrisk}[Transport can encode observer synchronization]
The transport map \(T_{\Gamma\to\Gamma'}\) can still encode a synchronization
rule, connection, conformal scale, or target proper-time comparison if the
shared-support relation and holonomy class are chosen for benchmark behavior.
Nontrivial holonomy is not a problem by itself. Selecting or hiding holonomy
to recover target clock comparison is the smuggling risk.
\end{smugglingrisk}

\begin{promotionblocker}[Clock-normalization blocker]
Candidate reconstruction remains blocked until A4 supplies a source-fixed
transport law or a failure-tolerant holonomy survey. A transported source
cycle is not yet an effective metric clock.
\end{promotionblocker}

\section{Audit Verdict}

\begin{auditverdict}[Transparent extension, unresolved target import]
The A1--A4 packet passes a narrow declaration audit: it explicitly labels its
new structures as ontology-extension hypotheses and does not directly install
target metric, target null-cone, radar protocol, target proper time, or fixed
rank four. It fails the stronger promotion-readiness audit. The unresolved
smuggling channels are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item finite source presentation choices can select a target-like
    localization arena;
    \item \(\Step\) can encode target topology through edge incidence;
    \item \(\mathcal{P}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\) can tune first-response ranks;
    \item \(\RespAlg\) quotient freedom can manufacture target rank; and
    \item \(\mathcal{K}\) and \(T_{\Gamma\to\Gamma'}\) can import clock
    normalization or observer synchronization.
\end{enumerate}
\end{auditverdict}

This is not a rejection of the research program. It is a local negative audit
for candidate reconstruction from the present axiom packet.

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Refuter benchmark-independence test]
The logical next role is a Refuter AgentJob that tests whether A1--A4 are
source-internally necessary and benchmark-independent. The test should ask
whether localization-relevant outputs are invariant across admissible finite
presentations, transition germs, discriminator families, response-algebra
quotients, and clock-transport holonomy classes. If they are not invariant,
the result should be preserved as a negative control result or routed to a
separate ontology-extension proposal, not to candidate reconstruction.
\end{nextburden}

Candidate Constructor and Gate Chair review remain premature.
