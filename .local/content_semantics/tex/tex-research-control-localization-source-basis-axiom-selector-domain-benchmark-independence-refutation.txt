\title{\textbf{Localization Source-Basis Axiom Selector-Domain Benchmark-Independence Refutation}\\[0.35em]
\large Invariance Test for Repaired Survey Domains \(D_0\)--\(D_F\)}
\author{Refuter AgentJob AJ-RT-20260608-018-001}
\date{June 9, 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This draft tests whether the selector-domain repair layer
\((\Dom_0,\ldots,\Dom_4,\Dom_F)\) establishes benchmark-independent
localization-relevant outputs. The result is local and negative. The repair
packet improves control state by requiring declared survey domains,
invariance criteria, and failure statuses before later candidate use.
However, as stated, it does not provide source-fixed finite enumerators,
closure-completeness proofs, or computed invariance ledgers for the repaired
domains. Admissible source-named choices can still disagree on presentation
boundaries, transition fronts, first-response ranks, rank spectra,
transported cycle ratios, holonomy reports, and status transitions.
Candidate reconstruction and Gate Chair review remain blocked.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Refutation Standard}

This artifact is a draft/control output for task \texttt{RT-20260608-018}.
It does not edit canonical ontology TeX, adopt A0--A4 or \(S_0\)--\(S_F\) as
canonical ontology, authorize candidate reconstruction, issue a Gate Chair
verdict, or reject the broader \TheoryProgram{}.

Let \(\Lang\) denote the registered source language before the A0--A4
extension sequence. Let the repaired selector package be
\[
S_i^{\mathrm{rep}}=(\Dom_i,\Inv_i), \qquad i\in\{0,1,2,3,4,F\}.
\]

\begin{test}[Repaired-domain benchmark independence]
The repair layer passes this local test only if each \(\Dom_i\) is fixed
before benchmark comparison, effectively enumerable from declared source
inputs, closed under the relevant source relabelings and refinements, and
paired with a recorded invariance ledger showing that localization-relevant
outputs are invariant across the full domain. The required outputs include
presentation boundaries, adjacency fronts, first-response ranks, response
rank spectra, transported cycle ratios, holonomy reports, and failure-status
transitions.
\end{test}

Declaring a survey domain is necessary. It is not sufficient. Benchmark
independence requires either actual invariance over the declared class or a
candidate claim formulated to survive the complete reported non-invariant
family.

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_0\)}{D0}: Seed, Closure, and Refinement Variation}

\begin{finding}[Domain naming does not fix the enumerator]
\(\Dom_0\) is defined as triples \((U,C,\rho)\) of bounded
source-accessibility seeds, source-order and source-flow closure operators,
and refinement policies. The repair packet requires all such triples within a
declared locality bound. It does not yet specify the source-fixed locality
bound, prove that the enumerator is complete, or show that all enumerated
triples are output-isomorphic.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Boundary divergence under source-named refinement]
For the same source records, one admissible closure can stop at immediate
source-flow support while another closes under a two-step source-flow shell.
One refinement can split records by exact support labels while another
refines by shared recurrence prefix. These choices can be declared before
benchmark comparison and remain source-named. They can still yield different
boundaries, adjacency-front inputs, and response-rank inputs.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Presentation-invariance not established]
\(\Dom_0\) fails the current benchmark-independence test. The packet states
the right survey obligation but does not yet supply a finite survey ledger or
a theorem proving presentation-output invariance across all admissible
\((U,C,\rho)\).
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_1\)}{D1}: Support-Equivalence Variation}

\begin{finding}[Support classes remain an underdetermined selector]
\(\Dom_1\) surveys support-equivalence relations and minimality tests
compatible with the presentation domain. The packet excludes target topology,
target observer protocols, and benchmark success. It does not prove that the
source records uniquely determine the support-equivalence class or that all
admissible classes induce the same transition fronts.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Front divergence under exact and prefix support]
An exact recurrence-word support relation can split a transition into several
minimal steps. A prefix support relation can merge the same records into a
coarser direct transition. Both relations can be source-defined and declared
before benchmark comparison. Their induced neighborhoods and fronts need not
agree.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Transition-front invariance not established]
\(\Dom_1\) cannot support candidate reconstruction until transition-front
data are shown invariant across the admissible support-equivalence and
minimality-test domain, or until non-invariant fronts are preserved as a
negative control family.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_2\)}{D2}: Discriminator-Closure Variation}

\begin{finding}[Closure over a family is not stability of the family]
\(\Dom_2\) closes over source-local perturbations and discriminators
generated by recurrence words, finite transition windows, chain-internal
response distinctions, and edit rules. The packet requires thresholds and
windows to be declared before benchmark comparison. It does not yet prove
that the closure generator is source-unique or that first-response ranks are
stable across the full closure.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Rank divergence under response windows]
One discriminator can fire on the first changed recurrence word; another can
fire only after a stable two-step response distinction. Both can be generated
from source records. They can assign different first-response ranks to the
same source pair.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[First-response-rank invariance not established]
\(\Dom_2\) fails the present test unless the full discriminator closure is
surveyed and all relevant \(r_D(a,b)\) values are stable. A discriminator
spectrum remains a negative control output unless a later claim is formulated
over the entire spectrum.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_3\)}{D3}: Quotient and Rank-Spectrum Variation}

\begin{finding}[Rank-spectrum invariance depends on quotient-domain completeness]
\(\Dom_3\) surveys response-word ground sets, recurrence equivalences,
source relabeling symmetries, and recombination quotients. The repair packet
correctly denies special status to a favorable rank in a non-singleton
spectrum. The unresolved issue is whether the admissible quotient domain is
complete and source-fixed.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Quotient divergence under recurrence equivalence]
One quotient can identify response functionals by recurrence equivalence.
Another can keep them independent when local supports differ. Both can be
source-named. The resulting rank spectrum can differ even when no rank value
is explicitly imported from a target benchmark.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Rank-spectrum invariance not established]
\(\Dom_3\) does not yet establish benchmark independence. A singleton rank
claim is usable only after the quotient-domain enumerator is fixed without
target information and the full admissible quotient class has been surveyed.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_4\)}{D4}: Transport and Holonomy Variation}

\begin{finding}[Transport-domain declaration does not fix clock comparison]
\(\Dom_4\) surveys shared-support relations, stable-cycle equivalences, and
transport maps. It excludes target synchronization and proper-time
normalization. It does not yet prove that exact-support, declared-overlap,
and refinement-compatible transport rules produce invariant cycle ratios and
holonomy reports.
\end{finding}

\begin{variation}[Transport-ratio divergence under overlap rules]
One rule can compare cycles only under exact support equality. Another can
compare cycles under declared-overlap support after refinement. Both rules
can be source-definable and can report holonomy honestly. They can still
produce different transported cycle ratios around the same source loop.
\end{variation}

\begin{blocker}[Clock-transport invariance not established]
\(\Dom_4\) remains benchmark-dependent at the current control level. A later
candidate may not select a target-compatible transport result from a
non-invariant transport family.
\end{blocker}

\section{\texorpdfstring{\(D_F\)}{DF}: Status-Ledger Variation}

\begin{finding}[Status transitions require survey evidence]
\(\Dom_F\) requires the surveyed domain, source inputs, target inputs
excluded, benchmark-comparison time, output family, invariance criterion, and
status. This prevents silent promotion only if the ledger is populated by an
actual survey. A rule allowing \(\mathsf{not\_computed}\) or
\(\mathsf{family\_valued}\) to become \(\mathsf{invariant}\) is not evidence
until the corresponding survey record exists.
\end{finding}

\begin{blocker}[Status-transition invariance not established]
The failure ledger is not benchmark-independent as a claim-supporting object
until status transitions are reproducible and tied to complete survey
records. Without those records, \(\mathsf{invariant}\) is an unaudited label.
\end{blocker}

\section{Local Verdict}

\begin{verdict}[Selector-domain repair fails the current benchmark-independence test]
The selector-domain repair packet is a genuine control improvement because it
makes the survey obligations explicit and blocks unsurveyed positive use. It
does not yet pass benchmark independence. The packet currently defines the
right repair surfaces but does not prove invariant localization-relevant
outputs across admissible source-defined domain variation. Candidate
reconstruction, canonical ontology adoption, benchmark promotion, and Gate
Chair review remain blocked.
\end{verdict}

This is a local negative result. It does not reject the \TheoryProgram{} and
does not reject selector-domain repair as a research object. It identifies
the missing evidence: source-fixed finite survey protocols, complete
enumeration records, and invariance ledgers for \(\Dom_0\)--\(\Dom_F\).

\section{Next Burden}

\begin{nextburden}[Finite survey protocol or controlled pause]
The logical next step is not Candidate Constructor and not Gate Chair review.
The next bounded task should either:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item route to an Ontology Formalizer to propose a finite survey protocol
    packet for \(\Dom_0\)--\(\Dom_F\), including source-fixed enumerators,
    closure-completeness criteria, and reproducible status-transition
    ledgers; or
    \item enter a Director-controlled pause if source-fixed enumerators
    require authority beyond the current draft/control extension lane.
\end{enumerate}
\end{nextburden}

Any future candidate must first show that its localization-relevant outputs
survive admissible variation in seed, closure, refinement, support
equivalence, discriminator closure, response quotient, transport, and
failure-ledger status.
